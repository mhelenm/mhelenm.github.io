Some of my work

I work in audio, film, and interactive media.

Poverty Puzzle: Economic Mobility

A video that looks at some of the effects of poverty in Chattanooga, for The Poverty Puzzle.

Poverty Puzzle:
Family Planning

A video that follows one woman looking back, and three friends looking forward, for The Poverty Puzzle.

Poverty Puzzle:
College Prep

A video that follows high school students on a college preparatory retreat, for The Poverty Puzzle.

Race in Chattanooga, now and 1950

An interactive map that shows where white and black people lived in 1950 and today.

Chattanooga Strong:
A city mourns

A video that mixes voicemail messages with the city's reaction to a shooting attack. It won a 2015 EPPY Award.

One Day
in Chattanooga

A crowd-sourced video project that documents one day in the life of the people of Chattanooga, Tenn.

Speak No Evil

A multimedia project about the cycle of violence in Chattanooga. The team I worked with was named as a 2014 Pulitzer Prize finalist in local reporting.

New South Docs

I'm the founder of a community of experimental documentary artists in Chattanooga. We tell true stories about the South in new ways.

Abortion in her words

An audio and animation project that presents the voices of four women, published before the vote on Tenn. Amendment 1.

The Toll

A web application that tracks all homicides in Chattanooga. It shows biographies of the victims and updates on their cases.

Ironman 2015
in Chattanooga

A video that shows competitors in the Ironman race and the supporters who came out to cheer them on.

Unfit

A project website with interactive graphics that I developed for a story about a family's struggle to keep a child from state custody.

Just Plumb Gone

A radio documentary that looks back on a deadly tornado. It won a regional Edward R. Murrow Award.

Women's coding group

I'm the founder and leader of CodeXX, a women's coding group in Chattanooga, Tenn.

An online guide for Tennessee state primaries and Hamilton County general elections.

About me

I am an independent audio and multimedia producer based in Chattanooga, Tenn., and an associate instructor at the Transom Story Workshop. I've won numerous national and regional awards, including a regional Edward R. Murrow Award for a radio news documentary, an EPPY Award for best use of social media, and first place awards from the Tennessee Associated Press for best multimedia and best website. I was also part of a four-person team named as a finalist for the Pulitzer Prize in Local Reporting in 2014.

I am the founder of CodeXX, a women's coding group in Chattanooga, and New South Docs, a group for experimental documentary makers.
